When to Conduct an Audit

Independent HR audits should be conducted at least once a year. Audits should also be triggered by:

  • Major organisational changes (mergers, restructuring)
  • Labour disputes or compliance concerns
  • Expansion into new markets or jurisdictions
  • Implementation of new HR systems or policies

Who Should Conduct an Audit

An independent HR audit must be conducted by a qualified, impartial, and experienced firm. OML Africa brings deep expertise in:

  • Labor law compliance across African jurisdictions
  • HR analytics, compensation benchmarking, and pay equity
  • Strategic policy design and digital audit tools
  • Sector-specific audits for high-risk industries

Our Audit Framework

From diagnosis to prescription, our process is structured, strategic, and delivers actionable insights.

1

Scope & Data Gathering

2

Key Benefits of an Independent Audit

Even the best internal audits can miss critical gaps. An independent review delivers tangible value.

  • Ensure Legal Compliance: Mitigate the risk of costly litigation and penalties by adhering to all local and international labor laws.
  • Uncover Hidden Inefficiencies: Identify operational bottlenecks, outdated processes, and biases that hinder productivity and growth.
  • Strengthen Governance & Transparency: Provide leadership and boards with an objective, data-backed view of workforce management.
  • Align HR with Strategic Goals: Ensure that your people strategy is not just a cost center, but a key driver of your organization's mission.
